# Standard Operating Procedure (SOP): Weekly Breakout Options Strategy

## 1. Objective & Scope

The purpose of this document is to outline the step-by-step rules and execution framework for the **Weekly Breakout Options Strategy**. This strategy is designed to capture sustainable, high-probability momentum shifts in large-cap stocks using weekly charts to eliminate market noise, while leveraging Deep In-The-Money (DITM) options to maximize returns and limit downside risk.

---

## Stage 1: Pre-Screening & Candidate Identification

Before evaluating a technical breakout, the underlying asset must meet specific structural, liquidity, and trend requirements.

* **Step 1.1: Liquidity Filter (Market Capitalization)**
* **Rule:** Restrict the trading universe exclusively to large-cap, well-established companies with a market capitalization exceeding **$25 Billion**.
* **Purpose:** Ensures tight bid-ask spreads and deep liquidity pools in the options market to avoid execution slippage.


* **Step 1.2: Long-Term Trend Verification**
* **Rule:** The stock must be in an established uptrend, strictly trading **above its 20-week moving average**.
* **Purpose:** Ensures trading aligns with the primary, long-term market momentum.


* **Step 1.3: Consolidation Identification (The "Box" Formation)**
* **Rule:** Locate assets experiencing a **minimum 6-week lateral consolidation**. The price action must form a clean horizontal "box" with distinct, easily identifiable support and resistance levels.


* **Step 1.4: Pre-Breakout Momentum Check**
* **Rule:** The near-term momentum must be positive. Confirm that the **MACD line is trading above its signal line** prior to the breakout.



---

## Stage 2: Breakout Confirmation

A position is only considered when a weekly candle conclusively breaks the established consolidation pattern with high conviction. Do not anticipate breakouts; wait for the weekly candle to close.

* **Step 2.1: Price Breakout Magnitude**
* **Rule:** The breakout weekly candle must close **at least 1% above the established resistance line** of the consolidation box.
* **Rule:** The breakout week must reflect an overall gain of **5% to 20%** compared to the previous week's close.


* **Step 2.2: Candle Structure & Buying Pressure**
* **Rule:** The breakout candle must close near the exact high of its weekly range. The upper wick must represent only a small fraction of the week's overall price movement.
* **Purpose:** Signals strong, sustained buying pressure into the weekly close.


* **Step 2.3: Multi-Week High Verification**
* **Rule:** The breakout price must establish at least a **10-week high** for the stock.


* **Step 2.4: Institutional Volume Validation**
* **Rule:** Trading volume during the breakout week must be **at least 30% higher** than the volume of the previous week.
* **Purpose:** Confirms institutional accumulation and participation rather than retail-driven volatility.



---

## Stage 3: Option Contract Selection & Capital Allocation

Do not trade common stock. Once the breakout is confirmed via Stage 2, options contracts are selected according to strict risk-mitigation metrics.

* **Step 3.1: Strike Price Selection (Delta & Intrinsic Value)**
* **Rule:** Purchase **Deep In-The-Money (DITM)** call options.
* **Prohibition:** Completely avoid At-The-Money (ATM) and Out-Of-The-Money (OTM) options. While they offer cheaper premiums, their high time-decay profiles introduce unacceptable risk of a 100% loss. DITM options mirror the stock price dollar-for-dollar and retain high intrinsic value.


* **Step 3.2: Expiration Selection (Time Horizon)**
* **Rule:** Select contracts with a minimum of **1 month to expiration**, though **2 months or longer is preferred** when liquidity allows.
* **Purpose:** Provides a buffer against time decay (theta) and gives the weekly macroeconomic swing room to develop.


* **Step 3.3: Position Sizing & Risk Parameters**
* **Rule:** Never risk more than **1% to 2% of total account equity** on a single trade.
* **Rule:** Because a standard 8–10% stop-loss in the underlying stock can translate to a ~4x amplified drop in option premium (~40–50%), **total capital allocation to the contract must not exceed 6% of total portfolio value**.



---

## Stage 4: Order Execution & Entry

* **Step 4.1: Entry Timing**
* **Rule:** Execute the order at the **following week's open** (Monday morning).


* **Step 4.2: Execution Price**
* **Rule:** Ensure the stock opens slightly above or at the breakout candle's closing price. Monitor the stock's immediate opening behavior and buy the chosen DITM option contract immediately to maintain momentum symmetry.



---

## Stage 5: Trade Management & Exit Rules

All exit management signals are monitored via the underlying stock's chart, but executed natively within the options contract.

* **Step 5.1: Initial Defensive Stop-Loss**
* **Rule:** Visually divide the horizontal consolidation "box" into three equal vertical sections (Upper 1/3, Middle 1/3, Lower 1/3).
* **Rule:** Place the stop-loss order at the **midpoint line of the middle section**. If the stock price reverses into the box and touches this midpoint, exit the options contract immediately.


* **Step 5.2: Trailing Stop for Profit Taking**
* **Rule:** Allow profits to run during a sustained move. Monitor the weekly MACD.
* **Trigger:** If a negative (bearish) crossover occurs (the MACD line crosses below the signal line), **mark the exact low price of that specific week**.
* **Action:** If the stock price subsequently falls below that marked weekly low, exit the entire position to secure profits.


* **Step 5.3: Contract Expiration & Position Rolling**
* **Rule:** If a trade has not triggered a technical exit but is nearing options expiration, the trader may manually close out the current contract and roll the position forward by purchasing an identical strike price with an extended expiration date. Be advised that this introduces additional transaction fees and slippage risks.
